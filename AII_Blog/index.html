VILLA TAPIA

A town with a great heart

Villa Tapia is a town in the Province of Hermanas Mirabal in the Dominican Republic. In 1862 a section of the municipality of La Vega called "La Jagua" was founded. The growth experienced by this section determined that in 1912 the first private institution was founded in said locality thanks to the diligence carried out by great personalities of the place. The section of La Jagua was elevated to the category of Municipal District on August 16, 1952 and its current name was given in honor of the memory of Mr. Doroteo Tapia. Villa Tapia is considered a municipality by law 293 of April 18, 1968. The first city council and the authorities were constituted on September 16, 1970 with the elections held on that date (official gazette No. 9772, April 20, 1968 )

For me, the best food you can eat in my town is delicious hamburgers. These hamburgers have become well known in the surrounding area, so every weekend many people from other towns come to Villa Tapia just to try and enjoy them.

Characteristics of my town

  • Villa tapia is one of the smallest towns in the Dominican Republic
  • The main crop of this town is the banana and other food
  • Villa Tapia only has about 20 thousand inhabitants
  • The climate is tropical, with strong periods of heat in the summer, like now.
  • It is a town with very friendly people and that preserves many traditions of the Dominican people

Although we are a small town, we have a big heart and every visitor is always more than welcome. We are always happy for you to come visit us.
